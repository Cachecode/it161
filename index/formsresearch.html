Forms

Forms are the only way to send data back to the server from a client. If there is a need to collect data from a user, writing an HTML form is the way. We can look at forms as a tool for a user to interact with a web page or application. You most often see forms coded in languages such as PHP, JAVA, and HTML.

Web forms ordinarily contain text boxes, texts areas, check boxes, dropdown lists, buttons, radio buttons, and hidden fields.

It is important to communicate logical and proper questions when writing forms, because a form is in most cases the pathway that allows users to communicate with the author, business, or what have you.

“Online forms are too important for any business website owner to ignore. They provide a means for potential customers to join your email list, ask about your products in a specific and personalized way, and give personal details useful to your business. Online forms allow users to buy your products remotely by filling their details and even making on-site payment. A website for a business without any web forms would be limited in terms of what it offers to potential customers. It would lack the mechanisms needed in today’s e-commerce.” - James George. Why Online Forms Are Important on Any Business Website. gsquaredstudios.com/online-forms/. Accessed 19 May 2021.

Having forms playing such a vital role on a webpage, it is important to implement good design. It is important when writing forms to keep it simple. You do not want to ask for too much information and make it easy for a user to follow. Make sure what you are asking is straightforward. The purpose of a form is to be clear. Radio buttons should be used when giving 2 or 3 choices to a user, a dropdown button should be used when listing 4 or more choices. Short answers should require a single-line text box and longer answers would need a multi-line text box. There should be more than enough room for a user to type their answer. These are the basics.
